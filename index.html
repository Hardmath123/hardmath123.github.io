I am a geek. When not staring at math problems or code, I'm producing, consuming, or criticizing music. Hardmath123 is my alias on the internet[1] [2] [3] [4]. It was chosen in a much-regretted burst of sponteniety when I was young and foolish, and has inexorably linked itself with me since then.

I write English and JavaScript. I'm interested in raytracing, neural networks, compiler design, cryptography, and web security. I believe in legible web design.

I care about CS education—I help write code for Berkeley Snap! and occasionally run CS events like Scratch Day in the Bay Area.

You're at my blog, an archive of thoughts, articles, stories, and tutorials. With Apologies to The Who,

I'm not tryin' to cause a big s-s-sensation,

I'm just bloggin' 'bout my g-g-generation.
